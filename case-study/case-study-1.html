Lunch Rush, No More Missed Orders

How one chicken rice stall reduced waste, avoided sell-outs, and served every customer — by trusting the numbers.

This story comes from a humble chicken rice stall that serves the lunch crowd in a busy neighborhood. For years, they faced the same daily dilemma: prepare too much and waste food, or prepare too little and turn customers away. The lunch hour was their peak — but also the most unpredictable part of their day.

They wanted to do better, but weren’t sure how.

We began by looking at their daily sales data, especially for two key items: steamed chicken rice and barley drinks. What we found surprised them. While steamed chicken rice was clearly their bestseller, its demand fluctuated wildly — some days they sold 12 plates, other days nearly 25. Barley, on the other hand, had more stable demand, usually between 4 to 6 glasses a day.

Using this information, we helped them forecast lunch demand more accurately. They started preparing 20–25 portions of chicken rice on weekdays, with a small buffer on Fridays and weekends, where demand typically spiked. For barley, they kept a consistent stock of 6–8 units, just enough to meet demand without overstocking.

They also noticed something else — most customers spent between RM16 and RM30, with the average around RM23. So, they introduced meal bundles priced at RM25–30, combining a main dish and drink. This not only encouraged slightly higher spending but helped them plan their prep more efficiently.

Thanks to these simple data insights, they saw:

  • 22% increase in average daily profit
  • Fewer last-minute sell-outs
  • Less food wasted at the end of the day
“We used to run out of chicken rice before 1 PM — now, we’re ready for the rush. Customers are happy, and we waste less.”

This isn’t just about spreadsheets. It’s about helping a small business serve every customer well, grow confidently, and breathe a little easier at the end of a busy day.
