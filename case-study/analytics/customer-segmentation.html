What Men and Women Buy

How customer segmentation revealed gender-specific purchasing patterns and unlocked new campaign strategies

At a Glance - Key Results

  • Identified distinct purchasing preferences by gender across 12 product categories
  • Increased targeted campaign conversion rates by 45%
  • Reduced marketing waste by 30% through precise audience targeting
  • Generated RM120,000 additional revenue through personalized strategies

Client Context

A growing online retailer specializing in lifestyle and consumer goods with a diverse product catalog. The company had been using broad marketing campaigns but suspected that men and women had different purchasing preferences that could be leveraged for better targeting.

Problem Statement

The retailer faced challenges with generic marketing approaches:

  • One-Size-Fits-All Campaigns: Same marketing messages sent to all customers regardless of preferences
  • Low Conversion Rates: Generic campaigns yielding poor engagement and sales
  • Wasted Ad Spend: Promoting irrelevant products to wrong audience segments
  • Unknown Preferences: No insights into customer behavior patterns by demographics

Our Approach & Key Findings

We analyzed 12 months of purchasing data across 12 product categories, segmenting customers by gender to identify distinct preference patterns. The analysis revealed significant differences in purchasing behavior that enabled targeted marketing strategies.

Business Impact

Measurable Outcomes:

  • 45% increase in targeted campaign conversion rates
  • 30% reduction in marketing waste through precise targeting
  • RM120,000 additional revenue from personalized product recommendations
  • 25% improvement in customer engagement metrics

Takeaways for Other Businesses

Customer segmentation can help your business:

  • Create highly targeted marketing campaigns
  • Improve product recommendation engines
  • Optimize inventory based on customer preferences
  • Increase conversion rates through personalization
  • Reduce advertising waste through better targeting
