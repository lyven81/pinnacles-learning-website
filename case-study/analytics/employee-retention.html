Before They Leave

How predictive analytics helped a tech company identify at-risk employees and reduce turnover by 35%

At a Glance - Key Results

  • Reduced employee turnover by 35% through proactive intervention
  • Identified top 5 factors predicting employee resignation risk
  • Saved RM250,000 annually in recruitment and training costs
  • Achieved 80% accuracy in predicting employees likely to leave

Client Context

A growing Malaysian tech company with 150+ employees was experiencing high turnover rates (25% annually). The HR team was reactive, only addressing retention after employees submitted resignation letters, making it too late for intervention.

Problem Statement

The company faced critical HR challenges:

  • Silent Departures: Employees leaving without warning signs detected
  • High Turnover Costs: RM15,000+ per employee for recruitment and training
  • Knowledge Loss: Key talent taking institutional knowledge with them
  • Reactive HR: No early warning system for at-risk employees
  • Unknown Risk Factors: Unclear what drives employee dissatisfaction

Key Insights & Predictions

Our predictive model identified the top risk factors for employee turnover and created an early warning system that achieved 80% accuracy in predicting which employees were likely to resign within 6 months.

Business Impact

Measurable Outcomes (12 months post-implementation):

  • 35% reduction in employee turnover rate
  • RM250,000 saved in recruitment and training costs annually
  • 80% accuracy in predicting at-risk employees
  • 90% retention rate for employees identified as at-risk who received intervention
  • 40% improvement in employee satisfaction scores

Takeaways for Other Businesses

If you're struggling with employee retention, predictive analytics can help you:

  • Identify at-risk employees before they decide to leave
  • Understand the real drivers of employee dissatisfaction
  • Create targeted retention strategies for high-value talent
  • Reduce recruitment and training costs significantly
  • Improve overall workplace culture and satisfaction
  • Make data-driven HR decisions rather than gut-feeling choices
